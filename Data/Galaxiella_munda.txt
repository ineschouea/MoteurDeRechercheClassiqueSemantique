'''''Galaxiella munda''''' is a species of [[fish]] in the [[Galaxiidae]] family. It is [[Endemism|endemic]] to inland waters of southwest [[Western Australia]], and known as the '''Western mud minnow'''.  
In 1999 the Mud Minnow was not included in the list of threatened species under the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act, but is listed as near threatened on the [[IUCN Red List]].<ref name=fishes />

== Description ==
This species has a maximum length of {{convert|6|cm|in|0}}.<ref name="FishBase"/> The Mud Minnow has an elongated scaleless body, a white belly and a grey-brown back. A distinctive broad brown stripe starts from behind the eye and ends tailfins base.
The fish has a one-year life-cycle.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.environment.gov.au/biodiversity/threatened/species/pubs/galaxiella-munda-advice.pdf|title=Advice to the Minister of Environment and Heritage from TSSC|year=2006|accessdate=2007-10-02}}</ref>

A number of informal names exist for this species, Galaxias, the Western dwarf galaxias, and Western mud minnow. The name '''mud minnow''' also refers to the [[Lepidogalaxiidae]] family, while '''Swan galaxias''' can refer to either ''Galaxiella munda'' or the ''[[Galaxias]]'' species ''[[Galaxias fontanus|G. fontanus]]''.<ref name="FishBase">{{FishBase species alt|9464}}</ref>

==Habit==
This species is only found in the South West corner of Western Australia, preferring swift-flowing streams in Karri forest.<ref name=fishes /> It inhabits coastal rivers, streams, ponds, swamps and ditches between Albany and Ellen Brook. It can tolerate acidic water that is tannin stained and with a pH as low as 3.0.

== Diet ==
''Galaxiella munda'' is carnivorous and mainly feeds on small insects, aquatic insect larvae and micro-crustaceans.<ref name=fishes>{{cite web|last1=Bray|first1=Dianne|last2=Gomon|first2=Martin|title=Western Dwarf Galaxias, Galaxiella munda|url=http://www.fishesofaustralia.net.au/home/species/3683|website=Fishes of Australia|accessdate=6 October 2014}}</ref>
