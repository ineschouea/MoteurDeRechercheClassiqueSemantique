'''Headis''' is a mix of [[table tennis]] and the heading of [[association football]]. It is played at a regular table tennis table so it combines tactical elements of table tennis and the legwork of [[tennis]]. In summer 2016 the 11th Headis World Championship was held with players from twelve countries.<ref>{{citation|periodical=sport.de|title=Headis: Mit dem Kopf und mit Köpfchen|language=German|url=http://www.sport.de/news/ne2327438/headis-mit-dem-kopf-und-mit-koepfchen/|access-date=2017-03-22
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Das Fest des Jahres! Die Headis Weltmeisterschaft 2016! • HEADIS|date=2016-07-28|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/2016/07/das-fest-des-jahres-die-headis-weltmeisterschaft-2016/|access-date=2017-03-22
}}</ref>

== History ==
The sport Headis (head + the ending of tennis) was invented in 2006 by the at that time sport student René Wegner. A football pitch in [[Kaiserslautern]], [[Germany]] was occupied but the table tennis table was available so they started playing a rubber ball only with their heads.

== Rules and gameplay ==
Two players play at a regular table tennis table and the ball must only make contact with the head. The table, however, can be touched by any part of your body. Playing a volley is allowed as well. After every ball played you have to touch the ground before heading the next ball.

Sets are played to eleven (11) points, with the exception that players must win by two (2) points.  Games are decided by best of three (3) sets.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Regeln • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/regeln/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref>

== Equipment ==
* Table tennis table: Headis is played at a regular table tennis table. The measurements are the same. As the Headis ball is heavier than a table tennis ball the net is made of metal so it is more stable.
* Headis ball: Headis is played with a special ball. It consists of rubber, weighs about {{convert|100|g|oz}} and has a [[circumference]] of {{convert|50|cm|in}}.<ref>{{cite web|title=Headis: Spieler springen kopfvoran auf die Platte – WELT|trans-title=|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.welt.de/regionales/nrw/article146211142/Spieler-springen-kopfvoran-auf-die-Platte.html|deadurl=|format=|accessdate=2017-03-22|archiveurl=|archivedate=|last=|date=|year=|month=|day=|language=German|pages=|quote=}}</ref>

== Tournament Series  ==

=== Setup of the Tournament Series ===
Every year there are ten to twelve Headis World Cup Tournaments held.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Geschichte • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/geschichte/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref> The results of these tournaments make the Headis World Ranking.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/rangliste-2/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref>

=== Player names ===
The players don’t use their regular names at the tournaments. They choose names like "Headi Potter" or "Rolli der Schlächter".<ref>{{cite web|title=Headis – Tischtennis mit dem Kopf: Wenn 'Headi Potter' und 'Rolli der Schlächter' um Titel kämpfen|trans-title=|periodical=|publisher=|url=http://www.rp-online.de/sport/andere/headis-wenn-headi-potter-und-rolli-der-schlaechter-um-titel-kaempfen-aid-1.5140314|deadurl=|format=|accessdate=2017-03-23|archiveurl=|archivedate=|last=RP ONLINE|date=|year=|month=|day=|language=German|pages=|quote=}}</ref>

=== World ranking ===
The Headis World Ranking is compiled by the results of the Headis World Cup Tournaments. The players receive World Ranking points according to their ranking at the tournaments. The bigger the tournament, the more points the players get. The currently last 15 tournaments build the Ranking.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/rangliste-2/|access-date=2017-03-08
}}</ref> As of May 2017, the two top players in the World are Cornelius "Headsinfarkt" Döll (1624 points) and Margarita "Klausi" Marmol Fernandez (890 points).<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/rangliste/|access-date=2017-03-08
}}</ref>

== History ==
The sport Headis (head + the ending of tennis) was invented in 2006 by the at that time sport student René Wegner. A football pitch in [[Kaiserslautern]], [[Germany]] was occupied but the table tennis table was available so they started playing a rubber ball only with their heads.

== Rules and gameplay ==
Two players play at a regular table tennis table and the ball must only make contact with the head. The table, however, can be touched by any part of your body. Playing a volley is allowed as well. After every ball played you have to touch the ground before heading the next ball.

Sets are played to eleven (11) points, with the exception that players must win by two (2) points.  Games are decided by best of three (3) sets.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Regeln • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/regeln/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref>

== Equipment ==
* Table tennis table: Headis is played at a regular table tennis table. The measurements are the same. As the Headis ball is heavier than a table tennis ball the net is made of metal so it is more stable.
* Headis ball: Headis is played with a special ball. It consists of rubber, weighs about {{convert|100|g|oz}} and has a [[circumference]] of {{convert|50|cm|in}}.<ref>{{cite web|title=Headis: Spieler springen kopfvoran auf die Platte – WELT|trans-title=|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.welt.de/regionales/nrw/article146211142/Spieler-springen-kopfvoran-auf-die-Platte.html|deadurl=|format=|accessdate=2017-03-22|archiveurl=|archivedate=|last=|date=|year=|month=|day=|language=German|pages=|quote=}}</ref>

== Tournament Series  ==

=== Setup of the Tournament Series ===
Every year there are ten to twelve Headis World Cup Tournaments held.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Geschichte • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/geschichte/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref> The results of these tournaments make the Headis World Ranking.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/rangliste-2/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref>

=== Player names ===
The players don’t use their regular names at the tournaments. They choose names like "Headi Potter" or "Rolli der Schlächter".<ref>{{cite web|title=Headis – Tischtennis mit dem Kopf: Wenn 'Headi Potter' und 'Rolli der Schlächter' um Titel kämpfen|trans-title=|periodical=|publisher=|url=http://www.rp-online.de/sport/andere/headis-wenn-headi-potter-und-rolli-der-schlaechter-um-titel-kaempfen-aid-1.5140314|deadurl=|format=|accessdate=2017-03-23|archiveurl=|archivedate=|last=RP ONLINE|date=|year=|month=|day=|language=German|pages=|quote=}}</ref>

=== World ranking ===
The Headis World Ranking is compiled by the results of the Headis World Cup Tournaments. The players receive World Ranking points according to their ranking at the tournaments. The bigger the tournament, the more points the players get. The currently last 15 tournaments build the Ranking.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/rangliste-2/|access-date=2017-03-08
}}</ref> As of May 2017, the two top players in the World are Cornelius "Headsinfarkt" Döll (1624 points) and Margarita "Klausi" Marmol Fernandez (890 points).<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/rangliste/|access-date=2017-03-08
}}</ref>

== History ==
The sport Headis (head + the ending of tennis) was invented in 2006 by the at that time sport student René Wegner. A football pitch in [[Kaiserslautern]], [[Germany]] was occupied but the table tennis table was available so they started playing a rubber ball only with their heads.

Die Headis Weltmeisterschaft 2016! • HEADIS|date=2016-07-28|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/2016/07/das-fest-des-jahres-die-headis-weltmeisterschaft-2016/|access-date=2017-03-08
}}</ref><ref>{{citation|periodical=Headis European Championship|title=die deutsche Qualifikation – Headis European Championship|date=2015-03-29|language=German|url=http://www.headis-ec.com/de/die-deutsche-quali/|access-date=2017-03-08

The [[1. FC Kaiserslautern]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Abteilungen: 1. FC Kaiserslautern|trans-title=|periodical=|publisher=|url=http://fck.de/de/1-fc-kaiserslautern/der-fck/vereinsstruktur/abteilungen.html|deadurl=|format=|accessdate=2017-03-08|archiveurl=|archivedate=|last=|date=|year=|month=|day=|language=German|pages=|quote=}}</ref> and the [[SV Darmstadt 98]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Headis: Darmstadt 98|trans-title=|periodical=|publisher=|url=http://sv98.de/home/verein/abteilungen/headis/|deadurl=|format=|accessdate=2017-03-08|archiveurl=|archivedate=|last=SV Darmstadt 1898 e.&nbsp;V.|date=|year=|month=|day=|language=German|pages=|quote=}}</ref> offer regular training.

== Rules and gameplay ==
Two players play at a regular table tennis table and the ball must only make contact with the head. The table, however, can be touched by any part of your body. Playing a volley is allowed as well. After every ball played you have to touch the ground before heading the next ball.

Sets are played to eleven (11) points, with the exception that players must win by two (2) points.  Games are decided by best of three (3) sets.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Regeln • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/regeln/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref>

== Equipment ==
* Table tennis table: Headis is played at a regular table tennis table. The measurements are the same. As the Headis ball is heavier than a table tennis ball the net is made of metal so it is more stable.
* Headis ball: Headis is played with a special ball. It consists of rubber, weighs about {{convert|100|g|oz}} and has a [[circumference]] of {{convert|50|cm|in}}.<ref>{{cite web|title=Headis: Spieler springen kopfvoran auf die Platte – WELT|trans-title=|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.welt.de/regionales/nrw/article146211142/Spieler-springen-kopfvoran-auf-die-Platte.html|deadurl=|format=|accessdate=2017-03-22|archiveurl=|archivedate=|last=|date=|year=|month=|day=|language=German|pages=|quote=}}</ref>

== Tournament Series  ==

=== Setup of the Tournament Series ===
Every year there are ten to twelve Headis World Cup Tournaments held.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Geschichte • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/geschichte/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref> The results of these tournaments make the Headis World Ranking.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/rangliste-2/|access-date=2017-03-23
}}</ref>

=== Player names ===
The players don’t use their regular names at the tournaments. They choose names like "Headi Potter" or "Rolli der Schlächter".<ref>{{cite web|title=Headis – Tischtennis mit dem Kopf: Wenn 'Headi Potter' und 'Rolli der Schlächter' um Titel kämpfen|trans-title=|periodical=|publisher=|url=http://www.rp-online.de/sport/andere/headis-wenn-headi-potter-und-rolli-der-schlaechter-um-titel-kaempfen-aid-1.5140314|deadurl=|format=|accessdate=2017-03-23|archiveurl=|archivedate=|last=RP ONLINE|date=|year=|month=|day=|language=German|pages=|quote=}}</ref>

=== World ranking ===
The Headis World Ranking is compiled by the results of the Headis World Cup Tournaments. The players receive World Ranking points according to their ranking at the tournaments. The bigger the tournament, the more points the players get. The currently last 15 tournaments build the Ranking.<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/fragen/rangliste-2/|access-date=2017-03-08
}}</ref> As of May 2017, the two top players in the World are Cornelius "Headsinfarkt" Döll (1624 points) and Margarita "Klausi" Marmol Fernandez (890 points).<ref>{{citation|periodical=HEADIS|title=Rangliste • HEADIS|language=German|url=http://headis.com/de/rangliste/|access-date=2017-03-08
}}</ref>
