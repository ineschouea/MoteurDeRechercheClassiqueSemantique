The '''Mitchell gudgeon''' ('''''Kimberleyeleotris hutchinsi''''') is a species of fish in the family [[Eleotridae]] [[Endemism|endemic]] to the [[Kimberley (Western Australia)|Kimberley]] region of [[Australia]], where it is only known from the [[Mitchell River (Western Australia)|Mitchell River]] system.  This species can reach a length of {{convert|4|cm|in|abbr=on}}.<ref>{{FishBase |genus= Kimberleyeleotris|species= hutchinsi| month = April | year = 2013}}</ref> The [[Specific name (zoology)|specific name]] honours the [[Ichthyology|ichthyologist]] [[J. Barry Hutchins]] (b. 1946) of the [[Western Australian Museum]], who collected the [[Type (biology)|type]].<ref name = ETYFish>{{cite web | author1 = Christopher Scharpf | author2 = Kenneth J.
